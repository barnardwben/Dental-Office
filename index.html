Why are regular dental exams a necessity?

Over time plaque and residue builds up around your teeth and gums. It can start to eat away at the surface and dig deeper into the roots of your teeth causing Gum Disease. When this happens it can lead to a plethora of serious health problems.

Going to the dentist for routine check-ups can prevent you and your family from these future health problems. It's important to wait no longer than 6 months between visits.

At our practice, we take you through a care plan that will help you achieve optimal health. Our dental hygienists start by cleaning the buildup around your teeth, and Dr. Ho will then thoroughly examine your mouth for any signs of untreated conditions. Once your examine is finished you'll have the relief of better understanding how to go about taking care of your mouth, as well as a team to help you maintain a healthy plan.

Do teeth need fluoride?

Flouride is useful for strengthening your teeth and preventing them from decaying. Adults get the benefits of protecting their teeth and it's encouraged by the American Dental Association (ADA), as well as the CDC that children should use fluoride toothpaste for brushing.

How can I fix my teeth and smile?

With technology advancing, it is now easier than ever to achieve the smile of your dreams and there are a variety of options one can choose from to get there. Some methods for improving ones overall dental appearance involve getting caps and crowns to cover problem teeth. There is also the option to add in veneers to your natural teeth.

Having a consultation with your dentist is the best solution to finding out your options. That is why we encourage you to visit our practice to schedule a consult with our team.

Are sweets really bad for my teeth?

Sugar from sweets can lead to cavities, and descrease the strength of you gums and teeth. Being conscious about the things you eat is the best way to prevent any damage to your health.

While teeth are strong enough to chew hard candies and sticky sweets, this can also lead to broken teeth and stress to your jaw. We encourage you to be cautious with the different things you eat so that you can prevent any injuries to your teeth and mouth.
